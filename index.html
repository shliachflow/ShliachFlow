The Meaning of Chanukah

The Story of Dedication

Chanukah, the Festival of Lights, commemorates the victory of the Jewish people over the Syrian-Greeks in the second century BCE. It celebrates the dedication of the Holy Temple in Jerusalem and the miracle of the oil that lasted for eight days instead of one. This history symbolizes the triumph of spiritual light over physical oppression.

The Symbolism of Light

The Menorah (or Chanukiah) is the centerpiece of the holiday. We light it for eight nights, increasing the light each night, to symbolize the ever-increasing presence of good and holiness in the world. The lights remind us that even a small amount of light can dispel a great deal of darkness, and that we must always increase in good deeds.

Bringing Light into the World

The core message is timeless: the power of faith and the dedication to one's principles can overcome seemingly insurmountable challenges.
